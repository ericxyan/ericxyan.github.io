# Concepts
## Distributed Applications
- Benefits
    - Reliability - Failure of a single or a few systems does not make the whole system fail.
    - Scalability - Performance can be increased as and when needed by adding more machines.
    - Transparency - Hide the complexity of the system and shows itself as a single entity.
- Challenges
    - Race condition - Two or more machines trying to perform operations on the same shared resources.
    - Deadlock - Two or more operations waiting for each other to complete indefinitely.
    - Inconsistency - Partial failure of data.
